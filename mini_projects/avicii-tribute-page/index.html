Avicii Tribute Page

Avicii

"I can't tell where the journey will end but I know where to start..."

Here's a time line of Avicii's life:

  • 1989 - Born in Stockholm, Sweeden, Tim Bergling, the son of Klas Bergling and actress Anki Lidén.
  • 2007 - Bergling signed on with the Dejfitts Plays label. He was a member of the Laidback Luke Forums, where he refined his craft and, at times, demonstrated his distinct deep house style.
  • 2010 - Releases the hit song "Seek Bromance", which reached the top 20 in several countries. The same year he signed with the European A&R team with EMI Music Publishing.
  • 2011 - Releases "Levels", which launched him into the mainstream. "Levels" reached the top ten in Austria, Belgium, Bosnia, Croatia, Denmark, Finland, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, the Netherlands, Slovenia and the United Kingdom, whilst topping the charts in Hungary, Norway and Sweden.
  • 2013 - The world premiere of Bergling's new single, "Wake Me Up". Same year Avicii was nominated for a Grammy for Best Dance Recording with "Levels" at the 2013 Grammy Awards. Bergling made his first South American tour, with concerts in Venezuela, Argentina, Colombia, Brazil and Chile.
  • 2014 - Bergling decided to cancel all of his remaining performances of 2014 due to health concerns. Releases his new single "The Days", a collaboration between Bergling and Robbie Williams
  • 2015 - Avicii performed live at Australia's Future Music Festival his upcoming single "Heaven", a collaboration with Coldplay frontman Chris Martin on vocals. The vocals were later resung by singer Simon Aldred from Cherry Ghost;
  • 2016 - Bergling performed live at Ultra Music Festival and premiered new IDs such as "Without You (feat. Sandro Cavazza)", "We Burn (Faster Than Light)", and a collaboration with Australian pop star Sia (which he first played in Dubai). On 29 March, Bergling announced on Facebook that he would be retiring from performing live and touring in 2016.[115] On 7 April 2016, Bergling announced that he was working on a third studio album.
  • 2017 - Bergling announced a documentary directed by his close and long time collaborator Levan Tsikurishvili, entitled Avicii: True Stories. The documentary chronicles the artist's retirement from touring and features interviews from his colleagues David Guetta, Tiësto, Wyclef Jean, Nile Rodgers and Chris Martin of Coldplay.
  • 2018 - Bergling released "Ghost", a collaboration with Swedish singer-songwriter Daniel Adams-Ray, who was credited as HUMAN.
  • April 2018 - Bergling died April 2018 near Muscat at the age of 28. No cause of death was immediately given. A funeral service was held on 8 June at the Skogskyrkogården cemetery in Stockholm. He was buried at Hedvig Eleonora Church in June 2018.
